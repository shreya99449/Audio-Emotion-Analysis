What Do These Results Mean?

The emotion analysis detects various emotional states in the audio:

  • Happy: Positive emotional tone, often with higher pitch and energy.
  • Sad: Lower energy, sometimes with slower speech rate and lower pitch.
  • Angry: Higher energy, faster speech rate, and increased intensity.
  • Neutral: Balanced emotional tone, typically with steady pitch and rhythm.
  • Fearful: Often characterized by trembling voice, higher pitch, and irregular rhythm.

The scores represent the probability or intensity of each emotion detected in the audio file.
