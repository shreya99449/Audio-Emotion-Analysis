About Voice Analysis

Voice Characteristics
  • Pitch: The fundamental frequency of the voice (in Hz). Male voices typically range from 85-180Hz, female voices from 165-255Hz.
  • Speech Rate: Estimated syllables per second. Average conversational speech is around 4-6 syllables per second.
  • Energy: Overall loudness and intensity of the voice. Higher values indicate a more powerful voice.
  • Clarity: Voice clarity based on noise/harmonic ratio. Higher values indicate clearer articulation.
  • Tone Variation: Measures how much the voice pitch varies. Higher values suggest more expressive speech.
  • Harmonic Ratio: The balance between harmonic (tonal) and percussive (noisy) components. Higher values indicate a more musical, pure tone.
Emotion Analysis
  • Happy: Positive emotional tone, often with higher pitch, energy, and speech rate.
  • Sad: Lower energy, sometimes with slower speech rate, lower pitch, and reduced tone variation.
  • Angry: Higher energy and intensity, often with increased speech rate and more sharp tonal attacks.
  • Neutral: Balanced emotional tone with moderate values across most metrics.
  • Fearful: Often characterized by trembling voice, irregular rhythm, and distinctive tonal patterns.
  • Surprised: Typically features sudden changes in pitch, wide bandwidth, and abrupt tonal shifts.
  • Disgusted: Often expressed with particular vocal patterns, including higher noise component and distinctive MFCC patterns.
  • Calm: Characterized by lower energy, slower speech, more harmonic tone, and narrower bandwidth.
  • Excited: Features fast speech, high energy, bright sound, and faster rhythm than neutral speech.
Visualizations Explained
  • Waveform: The amplitude (volume) of sound over time. Shows how loud or quiet the audio is at each moment.
  • Spectrogram: Shows frequency content of audio over time. Brighter colors represent stronger energy at that frequency.
  • MFCC: Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients represent vocal characteristics in a way similar to how humans perceive sound. These are powerful features for speech analysis.
Note: This analysis provides a simplified approximation of voice characteristics and emotions. In production environments, sophisticated machine learning models would be employed for more accurate results.
